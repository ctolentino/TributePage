Alice Catherine Evans

Championed the Pasteurisation of Milk

Alice Catherine Evans worked
at the U.S. Department of Agriculture
as a microbiologist

Timeline

  • 1881 - Alice Catherine Evans was born
  • 1901 - Taught Elementary School for 4 years
  • 1909 - Earned a BS in bacteriology from Cornell
  • 1909 - First woman to receive a bacteriology scholarship from the University of Wisconsin-Madison
  • 1910 - Earned a MS
  • 1910 - One of the first woman scientists to hold position at US Dept of Ag
  • 1918 - ID a bacterial infection carried by cows that could cause undulating fevers in humans
  • 1922 - Became infected with undulant fever and went through periods of illness and remission for 30 years
  • 1945 - Retired & motivated women in career development and science
  • 1975 - Died of a stroke at age of 94
